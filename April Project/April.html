THE STRING INSTRUMENTS FAMILY











VIOLIN




The violin is the soprano of the string family. The high register of the violin allows for cascading melodic lines that can supersede the other string instruments' register. The four strings that make up the violin are G-D-A-E








VIOLA




The viola is the middle-child of the string family. It looks like a violin but slightly bigger. The viola has the capability to meld together the high register of the violin with the lower trembles of the cellos and basses. It has a thicker sound than the violin, and some almost prefer it's mellow middle-register timbre. The strings that make up the viola are the same as a cello: C-G-D-A








CELLO




The cello is said to have the closest timbre to the human voice. Because of its popularity, there is a lot of repertoire written for the cello.
The strings that make up a cello are the same as viola C-G-D-A, but somehow the two instruments sound completely different. The sheer size of the cello allows for a bigger chamber of sound to cumulate from within.










BASS




The string bass acts like the silent housekeeper working in the background in an orchestra. Really, you only notice it if someone messes up, but it makes a tremendous impact on the overall thickness and warmth of the sound. The bass' trembling resonance coats the cumulative sound and lays out the foundationi by playing the bass line.
The notes that make up a bass are E-A-D-G. Notice that it is the same notes but in opposite order from the violin.
